Schemes to reduce carbon emissions

Two types of scheme


There are two types of schemes to reduce carbon emissions, carbon tax and Emissions Trading Scheme.

In carbon tax, companies are basically taxed on the amount of emissions they produced. These are normally measured in tonnes of CO2e (Carbon dioxide equivalents). In ETS, companies are allocated carbon credits. This basically means that specific companies are allowed to produce a capped amount of CO2e emissions. If the capped amount is has been exceeded, the company will be charged with a high amount. This scheme allows companies to basically trade and sell their credits to other companies. So, companies that are under their emissions on a fiscal year can sell their credits to others who are as efficient as them.
